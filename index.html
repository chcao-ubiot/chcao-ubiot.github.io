Chenhong Cao

Chenhong Cao is an Associate Professor with the School of Computer Science and Technology, University of Science and Technology of China, where she is affiliated with the Ubiquitous Battery-free IoT<\b> Laboratory led by Prof. Wei Gong. She received her Ph.D. in Computer Science from Zhejiang University in 2018 under the supervision of Prof. Wei Dong and Academician Chen Chun, and holds B.S. and M.S. degrees in Computer Science from Northeastern University, China. Prior to her current appointment, she conducted postdoctoral research at Zhejiang University and served on the faculty of Shanghai University as Lecturer and later Associate Professor. Her research focuses on edge computing, IoT systems and networking, wireless and mobile computing, with particular emphasis on backscatter communication and passive IoT systems. Her work centers on system architecture, protocol, and physical-layer co-design, and performance optimization for scalable, battery-free intelligent sensing and connectivity. She has led multiple nationally funded projects as Principal Investigator, including grants from the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC), and has contributed as a key researcher to national high-technology programs. Dr. Cao has received several competitive research recognitions, including the Xiaomi Young Talents Program (2024), the Shanghai Science and Technology Young Talents Sailing Program (2020), and the Shanghai University Young Talents Sailing Program (2022). Her research aims to advance high-efficiency, sustainable, and large-scale networked intelligence infrastructures for next-generation ambient computing environments.
